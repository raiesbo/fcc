Arch. Rafael Guastavino i Moreno

"The architect of New York"

- The New York Times

Portrait of Rafael Guastavino

Here is Rafael Fuastavino's remarcable life:

  • 1842 - Born in Valencia, Spain.
  • 1881 - Guastavino moved to New York City from Valencia, with his youngest son, nine-year-old Rafael III.
  • 1884 - Finishes "The Dakota Apartment House".
  • 1885 - An article entitled "The Dakota Apartment House", printed in The Real Estate Record and Builders Guide, Guastavino was identified as the contractor in charge of "fireproof construction" of the luxury apartment building.
  • 1885 - Patents his "Tile Arch System" in New York, which was used for constructing robust, self-supporting arches and architectural vaults using interlocking terracotta tiles and layers of mortar.
  • 1889 - Was commissioned by the firm of McKim, Mead, and White for their Boston Public Library.
  • 1890 - After forming a partnership with William Blodgett, Guastavino eventually was offered a construction position with George W. Vanderbilt to construct arches for the new mansion, Biltmore at Asheville, North Carolina.
  • 1900 - New York architects Heins & LaFarge hired Guastavino to help construct City Hall station, the underground showpiece for the IRT, the first part of the then-new New York City Subway.
  • 1908 - dies in Asheville, North Caronlina, at the age of 65. He is buried in the crypt of the Basilica of St. Lawrence, Asheville, which he designed in 1905.
